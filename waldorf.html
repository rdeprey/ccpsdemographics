The Changing Demographics of Charles County Public Schools

In light of an August 2014 CNN article by Ruben Navarrette that speaks about America's changing demographics in public schools, the below is a study on the same topic within this Southern Maryland county. The data visualization and short blurbs below will provide clarity on the breakdown at a county level and by each school.


Demographics at the county level

The student body in Charles County public schools has seen an increase in minorities from the '02-03 school year to '11-12 school year and overall student enrollment has slightly increased within that ten year span. The African-American and Hispanic population has seen a 43% and 150% rise, respectively, in that period. The white population, in that same time frame, has decreased 33% and had gradual decreases year over year.

Hover over the chart to explore the overall demographics breakdown.


Demographics by each schools

Of the 36 schools in Charles County, 26 of them entered the '12-13 school year with non-Hispanic white students as a minority group. Of those schools, only two (Smallwood Middle and Matula Elementary) had an African-American student population of less than 50%. 19 schools had at least five percent of their student body represented with Hispanic background entering that same school year, a stark contrast to only one (Middleton Elementary) in '02-03.

Note: This analysis includes schools and programs listed under the Charles County Public School System. "Other" in the analysis includes the following: American Indian/Alaska Natives, Asian or Asian/Pacific Islanders, Hawaiian Native/Pacific Islanders, and Multi-racial students. Sources: National Center for Education Statistics
